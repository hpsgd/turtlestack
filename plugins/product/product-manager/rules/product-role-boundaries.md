# Product role boundaries — distinct disciplines that look overlapping

Some product disciplines look like duplicates and get collapsed into one role or one skill. That collapse loses real value. This rule names the boundaries that must hold, and the principle behind them: overlapping lenses on a shared topic are a feature, not redundancy to be reconciled.

## Product manager vs product owner

These are not the same job. As Melissa Perri puts it: the Product Owner is a role you play on a Scrum team; the Product Manager is the job.

- **Product manager** owns the why and the what: problem validation, continuous discovery, JTBD, roadmap, PRDs, RICE prioritisation, and strategy input to the CPO.
- **Product owner** owns the how-we-ship-it: backlog grooming, user stories, story maps, acceptance-criteria readiness, sprint readiness. The PM is upstream.

When the two collapse into one role under delivery pressure, discovery is the first thing to die — the role becomes a backlog-administration function feeding a feature factory. The [feature factory](https://cutle.fish/blog/12-signs-youre-working-in-a-feature-factory) (John Cutler) is the failure mode: shipping output with no validated connection to outcomes. Keep the roles distinct so discovery has an owner.

Do not re-merge them. A future agent or skill that hands PRDs, roadmap, or discovery back to the product owner has collapsed the split.

## Overlapping disciplines are intentional (multi-lens)

Several disciplines are deliberately held by more than one role, each with its own lens. This is by design — different vantage points produce different reads, and the conflict between them is information. Do not reconcile, rename, or merge them.

- **Competitive analysis** lives in two places: the GTM team's `competitive-analysis` is sales-enablement-flavoured (how we win deals against this competitor); the research analyst's `competitive-analysis` is strategic-CI-flavoured (what this competitor's moves mean for our strategy). Both are consulted on decisions that touch competitors. Keep both.
- **Voice of Customer** is distributed, not centralised: the product manager, UX researcher, support, customer success, GTM, and research analyst each hold their own VoC lens. There is no single VoC owner. The product owner consults the relevant lenses during grooming. When two lenses disagree, surface the disagreement — don't average it away.

The rule of thumb: when a discipline touches several roles, give each its own focused version and let the consuming role know to ask any or all of them. A single "owner" of a cross-cutting discipline flattens exactly the perspective differences that make the discipline worth consulting.
